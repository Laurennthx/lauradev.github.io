About Me

My name is Laura García Martín, and I am a fourth-year student of Videogame Design and Development at Rey Juan Carlos University.

I discovered my passion for technology later than usual, after spending a year studying Economics and International Business. That change of direction led me to rethink my future, retake the EBAU, and pursue what I truly wanted. Since then, I have never doubted that I made the right decision. I found great satisfaction exploring Unity’s Inspector, uncovering its possibilities, and developing a strong ambition for interface design and user experience.

My TFG

My project develops an accessible videogame for people with Parkinson’s, aimed to provide entertainment adapted to their motor and vocal skills. To ensure the privacy of voice data collected during gameplay, homomorphic encryption is implemented, allowing analysis without compromising security. The proposal combines entertainment, accessibility, and data protection, offering an innovative tool for digital therapy.

Implemented in Unity, with mechanics designed to improve motor skills and voice precision in players.
